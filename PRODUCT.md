# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Regular Minecraft players who want to play mods in their native language. They download modpacks from CurseForge or Modrinth, discover untranslated content, and need a simple tool to localize everything. They are not developers or technical translators — they want drag-and-drop simplicity, not configuration files.

## Product Purpose

MovaMC translates Minecraft mods between languages. Drop a folder of JAR files, pick a target language, get translated mods back. It automates the entire pipeline: extracting language files, translating with AI or Google Translate, validating placeholders so nothing breaks in-game, and repacking into ready-to-use JARs.

Success means a player runs MovaMC, clicks through a few steps, and gets a fully translated modpack in minutes — no editing JSON by hand, no broken `%s` placeholders, no configuration headaches.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, cozy, Minecraft-native.** The tool should feel like it belongs in the Minecraft ecosystem — not like enterprise software or a developer utility. Think torch light, not fluorescent office lighting. Think wooden planks and stone, not glass-and-steel SaaS.

Three words: **теплий, ламповий, рідний** (warm, cozy, familiar).

## Anti-references

- **SaaS dashboards** — no glassmorphism cards, no gradient hero sections, no metric grids with big numbers. This is a tool for players, not a startup analytics panel.
- **AI-generated dev-tool aesthetic** — no purple/indigo dark theme (the "Vercel clone"), no monospace-everywhere terminal cosplay, no aggressive neon accents. Warmth beats coldness.
- **Overly gamified** — no pixel-art UI, no blocky Minecraft font everywhere, no dirt/grass textures. Minecraft-native feel, not Minecraft cosplay.
- **Generic "modern minimalist"** — no flat gray-on-white boredom. The tool should have personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Minecraft-native, not Minecraft cosplay.** The interface should feel at home in the Minecraft world without literally copying block textures or pixel fonts. Warm materials, earthy tones, soft light.

2. **Disappear into the task.** The player opens the tool to translate mods, not to admire the UI. Every element should serve the workflow. Delight is in the smoothness, not the decoration.

3. **Warmth over coldness.** When choosing between two equally functional approaches, pick the warmer one. Amber over cyan. Rounded over sharp. Soft over harsh.

4. **Just works.** Zero configuration required for the happy path. Sensible defaults, auto-detection, one-click translate. Complexity is opt-in, never the default.

5. **Honest about AI.** The tool uses AI translation but shouldn't feel like an "AI product." No sparkle emoji, no robot mascots, no "powered by AI" badges. The AI is plumbing, not personality.
